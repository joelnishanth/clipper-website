Permissions

Clipper needs a few macOS permissions to work properly:

  • Microphone — records your voice during meetings and voice notes. You can revoke this anytime in System Settings → Privacy & Security → Microphone.
  • Screen Recording — captures system audio from meeting apps (audio only, no screen video). Grant in System Settings → Privacy & Security → Screen & System Audio Recording.
  • Notifications — optional alerts when Clipper detects a meeting.
  • Calendar — optional, for upcoming events and meeting detection. Enable in Clipper Settings → Connectors.

See the setup guide for step-by-step instructions.

Recording issues

  • No audio captured — check that Microphone and Screen Recording permissions are granted. Restart Clipper after granting permissions.
  • Meeting not detected — ensure meeting detection is enabled in Settings → Notifications. Calendar integration can improve detection accuracy.
  • Transcription slow or stuck — on-device models require Apple Silicon. Ensure AI models are downloaded in Settings → Models.

Data & privacy

Clipper stores all recordings, transcripts, and notes locally on your Mac. AI processing runs on-device — your personal content is not uploaded to any server.

Read our full privacy policy for details on data handling, network usage, and optional integrations.
